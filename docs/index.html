Chasing the Ceiling

An honest, fully-logged campaign to build a competitive Mahjong AI — and the research artifact it became: a forensic study of why in-house evaluation lies in imperfect-information games.

Behavior cloningSelf-play RLPIMC search Duplicate-format evalNon-transitivityNegative results

TL;DR. Our supervised-learning bot ranked ~14/69 (Sim-7) → 27/38 (Sim-8). We threw the whole playbook at it — RL, distillation, soups, search, advantage-weighted BC, even cloning the bots that beat us. Not one robustly beat the SL floor. The only change that demonstrably won points was a bug fix. The lasting deliverable is not a model but a trustworthy way to measure — and a pile of well-evidenced negative results that became a paper. Start with the Phase-1 Autopsy.

Milestones

2026-06-07
The one real win: a −30 bug
A preempted-CHI desync caused wrong-Hu declarations (−30 each) in Sim-7. Echo-confirmed claims fixed it; triple-verified with PHANTOM_HU=0 over 39,145 replays. Sim-7 rank: 14/69. The only demonstrable point-gain of the whole campaign was this fix, not a model.
2026-06-08
The standard playbook is exhausted — and eval is non-transitive
Full gauntlet vs the floor: ensembles, soups, champion-clone, raven — none beat it. Built an on-distribution gauntlet from the 39,145-game 2025 final and found the killer property: "which model is best" flips with the opponent pool. The bug-fixed zip is the only unambiguous must-ship.
2026-06-09
The breakthrough teacher + the RL verdict
Single-coherent-teacher distillation (lad_chunjiandu) beats the multi-teacher BC — coherence > diversity. Root-caused the eval-bench deadlock (missing sentinel + no read timeout). RL conclusively fails even with a diverse opponent pool: SL beats feasible-compute RL, replicating the PKU/Suphx finding.
2026-06-10
A trustworthy yardstick
The persistent-bot duplicate gauntlet: lad_chunjiandu +4119 > floor +3938 (144g) — the third independent eval favoring it. Value-of-resulting-state search edges +57/144g — promising but inside the noise floor (±537/144g for identical bots). Measurement, not modeling, is the bottleneck.
2026-06-14
Phase 1 closes — and three real results
Sim-8: 27/38. The last lever — distilling toward the strongest field bots — is a clean null (best ties). But the attempt produced three keepers: the "win8=0" RL wall was partly a scoring-bug (a null that was a typo); full-net warm-started RL is compute-infeasible (measured ~50 min/iter); and a self-contained field ranking (we sit below our own teacher; several top bots are LLM-API players). The negative results are the paper. See the Autopsy.

The paper

"The Evaluation Gap: Why In-House Benchmarks Fail to Predict Real-Field Performance in Imperfect-Information Games" (target: IEEE Transactions on Games). A failure taxonomy of how each in-house evaluation layer inverts ground truth, a controlled imitation-ceiling study, a noise-floor quantification, and a verified protocol + open toolkit.
